Instructions

Weight

Just step on your bathroom scale and record your weight. For best results, do this in the morning before eating anything.

Waist

Using a measuring tape, measure around your mid-section where the circumference is the smallest. Exhale, and don't suck in your gut. That's cheating!

Hips

With a measuring tape, measure around the mid-section where the circumference is largest. Like before, no cheating!

Upper Arm

With your elbow slightly bent, measure the circumference of your forearm at its largest point. Keep the arm relaxed. It doesn't matter which arm you measure, but use the same one every time (or average measurements from both arms).

Thigh Circumference

With a measuring tape, measure the circumference of your thigh at its largest point. Keep the muscles relaxed. Like arm measurements, either use the same leg each time, or average the measurements between both legs.

Chest Fat

Using calipers, take a measurement about a hand's-width below the right collarbone, and about 2 inches out from the midline of your chest. Try to be consistent about the location.

Belly Fat

With your calipers, take a measurement about an inch to the right of your belly button.

Thigh Fat

With calipers, take a measurement on the midline of your upper thigh. To help be consistent about the location, placing your thumb on the top of your hip bone, and reach your pinkie as far down the midline of your thigh as you can. Measure at that point.

General Points

  • Try to be consistent in your measurements
  • For best results, take 2 (or more) measurements and average them.

  • You may use any metric you wish. I used pounds for weight, cm for tape measurements, but feel free to use kg for weight, inches for tape measurements, etc. Just be consistent.

    For caliper measurements, you must use mm, or the body fat percentages will not be calculated correctly.
